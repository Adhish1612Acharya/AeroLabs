Overview

  • Aeromodelling involves creating and flying miniature aircraft models.
  • Introduces enthusiasts to principles of aerodynamics and mechanics.
  • A blend of science, engineering, and creativity.

History

  • Roots trace back to the early 1900s with simple gliders.
  • Evolved to include powered and radio-controlled aircraft.
  • A popular hobby among aviation enthusiasts.

Types of Aeromodelling

  • Free Flight: Flies freely without external control.
  • Control Line: Controlled via wires attached to the aircraft.
  • Radio Control: Operated using remote controllers.

Materials Used

  • Balsa wood: Lightweight and durable.
  • Foam: Easy to shape and cost-effective.
  • Carbon fiber: Provides strength with minimal weight.

Getting Started

  • Start with beginner kits for easier assembly.
  • Understand flight mechanics and basic aerodynamics.
  • Gradually experiment with advanced models and designs.
